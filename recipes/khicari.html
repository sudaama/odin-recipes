Khicari

Khichari is a nutritious stew mainly composed of dal and rice.

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup(85 ml) split mung beans
  • 1 cup (250 ml) basmati or other long grain rice
  • 3 tablespoons (60 ml) ghee or oil
  • 1/3 cup (85 ml) raw cashew pieces or halves
  • 2 teaspoons (10ml) cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon (20 ml) fresh hot green chilli, minced
  • 2 tablespoons (40 ml) minced fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon (5 ml) tumeric
  • 1 teaspoon (5 ml) yellow asafoetida powder
  • 1 small cauliflower (about 400g, or 14 ounces) cut into small flowerets
  • 5-6 cups (1.25 - 1.5 litres) water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons (7 ml) salt
  • 1 tablespoon (20 ml) butter
  • 2/3 (165 ml) cooked green peas
  • 1 cup (250 ml) tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup (125 ml) chopped fresh coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. Wash and drain the dal and rice.
  2. Heat the ghee in a heavy 4-litre non-stick saucepan over moderate heat.
  3. Fry cahews in hot ghee until they turn golden brown, then put aside
  4. Fry the cumin seeds in the ghee, then add the chillies and ginger, then saute and add the tumeric and asafoetida
  5. Add cauliflower pieces, stir fry for a minute or so.
  6. Finally, add dal and rice, stirrin in the spices and vegetables for 1 minute.
  7. Add water and bring to full boil over high heat
  8. Reduce heat to low, partially cover and cooke slowly for about 25 - 30 min until the dal and rice are soft.
  9. Before removing the khichari from heat, fold in the salt, butter, cooked green peas, chopped tomatoes, toasted cashews and the chopped coriander leaves.
